# ISO 27001 — Relevant Controls for Vendor Assessment

Reference corpus for LEXIS semantic search. Expand during Day 2.

---

## A.5 — Information security policies

Policies approved by management, published, communicated, and reviewed at planned intervals.

## A.6 — Organization of information security

Roles and responsibilities defined. Segregation of conflicting duties. Contact with authorities and special interest groups.

## A.8 — Asset management

Inventory of assets. Acceptable use of assets. Return of assets upon termination. Classification and labeling of information.

## A.9 — Access control

Access control policy. User registration and de-registration. Privileged access management. Password management. Review of user access rights.

## A.10 — Cryptography

Policy on use of cryptographic controls. Key management procedures.

## A.12 — Operations security

Documented operating procedures. Protection from malware. Backup procedures. Logging and monitoring. Clock synchronization. Installation of software on operational systems. Management of technical vulnerabilities.

## A.13 — Communications security

Network security management. Segregation in networks. Information transfer policies. Agreements for information transfer. Confidentiality or non-disclosure agreements.

## A.14 — System acquisition, development and maintenance

Security requirements in information systems. Secure development policy. Test data protection. Change control procedures.

## A.16 — Information security incident management

Responsibilities and procedures for incident management. Reporting of information security events. Assessment and decision on events. Response to incidents. Learning from incidents. Collection of evidence.

## A.17 — Business continuity

Planning information security continuity. Implementing information security continuity. Verify, review, and evaluate continuity.

## A.18 — Compliance

Identification of applicable legislation and contractual requirements. Intellectual property rights. Protection of records. Privacy and protection of PII. Independent review of information security.

---

## Common gaps to flag in vendor contracts

- No ISO 27001 certification or Statement of Applicability referenced
- No incident response procedure or SLA for response time
- No vulnerability management or patch policy described
- No backup frequency or recovery time objective (RTO/RPO) specified
- No access control or privileged access management described
