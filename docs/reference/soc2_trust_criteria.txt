# SOC 2 Type II — Trust Service Criteria

Reference corpus for LEXIS semantic search. Expand during Day 2.

---

## CC1 — Control Environment

Demonstrates commitment to integrity and ethical values. Board exercises oversight. Management establishes structures, reporting lines, and authorities. Commitment to competence. Accountability for internal control.

## CC2 — Communication and Information

Uses relevant, quality information. Internally communicates information necessary for functioning of internal control. Communicates with external parties regarding matters affecting internal control.

## CC3 — Risk Assessment

Specifies suitable objectives. Identifies and analyzes risk. Considers potential for fraud. Identifies and assesses significant changes.

## CC6 — Logical and Physical Access Controls

Restricts logical access. Restricts physical access. Manages credentials for infrastructure and software. Manages credentials for data stores. Manages encryption keys.

## CC7 — System Operations

Manages configuration. Manages capacity. Implements controls to prevent or detect malicious software. Monitors infrastructure and software. Implements change management. Implements backup and recovery.

## CC8 — Change Management

Authorizes, designs, develops, configures, documents, tests, approves, and implements changes.

## CC9 — Risk Mitigation

Identifies, selects, and develops risk mitigation activities for risks from business disruptions and use of vendors.

## A1 — Availability

Maintains, monitors, and evaluates processing capacity and environmental protections. Identifies and addresses processing capacity and environmental protections.

## C1 — Confidentiality

Identifies and maintains confidential information. Disposes of confidential information.

## PI1 — Processing Integrity

Processing is complete, valid, accurate, timely, and authorized.

## Common gaps to flag in vendor contracts

- No SOC 2 Type II report available or referenced
- Report covers only Type I (point-in-time) not Type II (operating effectiveness)
- Scope of SOC 2 report does not cover the specific service being purchased
- No independent auditor name or report date provided
- Trust criteria gaps: no availability SLA, no confidentiality controls described
